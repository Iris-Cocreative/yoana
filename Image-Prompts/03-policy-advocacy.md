A flat editorial illustration for use as an icon. A single living tree, centered vertically on the canvas. The tree is stylized but unmistakably organic — all winding curves and varying line weights, like something from a vintage naturalist field guide or a hand-pressed botanical print.

The trunk is broad at the base and tapers upward, slightly asymmetrical. The roots spread wide below the soil line — they wind and branch irregularly, the way real roots do. They vary in thickness: thick near the trunk, tapering to fine tendrils at the tips. Some roots terminate in small teardrop or leaf-shaped forms, suggesting they are alive and reaching. A few root strands loosely encircle small oval forms — simplified seeds or people — as if the roots are holding community in the earth.

The canopy above is spare: four or five irregular branches extend from the upper trunk, each with a cluster of two or three minimal leaf shapes — single curved strokes, not botanical detail. The overall canopy suggests openness and possibility rather than density.

There are no right angles. No parallel lines. No grid. No rectangles. No circuit-board aesthetic. Every line winds slightly.

Background: warm cream (#fff6cf), flat.

Color palette: forest green (#2f5a4a) for the trunk, main branches, and roots. Lighter sage green (#6b8e5c) for leaves, fine root tendrils, and secondary branches. Umber (#4a2418) for thin outline strokes on the trunk. Background cream. No other colors.

Style: contemporary editorial illustration with vintage naturalist botanical-print sensibility. References old-growth tree drawings in handmade activist zines and social justice publications. Organic, warm, slightly imperfect linework — never mechanical.

Mood: law as a living, growing thing rooted in community. Change that takes time and goes deep. Systemic transformation, not quick fixes.

Avoid: circuit boards, right angles, grid patterns, chip diagrams, computer imagery, mechanical geometry, literal documents or scrolls, scales of justice, courthouse columns, clipart trees, symmetrical fractal branching, photorealism, 3D effects, gradients.
