A flat editorial illustration for use as an icon. At the center of the composition, a single smooth river stone — a solid oval form, river-worn and slightly irregular, warm and heavy-feeling. It sits still at the center of the frame. From it, four concentric oval rings radiate outward, like the surface of water when a stone is dropped into it — or like the circles of people that gather in a restorative justice process around a harm.

The stone and rings are the entire composition. Nothing else.

The rings are not mechanical circles — they are slightly organic ovals, each with minor irregularities in their curves, as if drawn by a steady but human hand. The innermost ring is close to the stone and slightly thicker. Each subsequent ring is progressively thinner and slightly more faint — not as a gradient, but as a thinning of line weight. The outermost ring nearly reaches the edges of the frame.

The stone itself is filled solid in warm amber (#ff8e3f). It has a single, very fine crack line — barely visible, a hairline — running diagonally across it, suggesting it has been through something. Not broken. Just marked.

The concentric rings are drawn in a lighter, warmer amber tone (#ffc465), thinning outward.

Background: warm cream (#fff6cf), flat and clean.

Color palette: amber (#ff8e3f) for the stone, lighter amber (#ffc465) for the rings, thin umber (#4a2418) line for the hairline crack. Background cream. No other colors.

Style: flat editorial illustration, centered and meditative. References both the concentric circle geometry of the Yoana Tchoukleva logo and the physical act of dropping a stone in still water. Suggests both the moment of harm (the stone that falls) and the circles of response, witness, and healing that ripple outward from it.

Mood: stillness after impact. The circles of a restorative process — held, widening, unhurried.

Avoid: human figures, circle of people, flames, candles, clipart, gavel, handcuffs, prison imagery, perfect mechanical circles, gradients, 3D effects, photorealism, shadows, new-age crystal aesthetics, symmetrical mandalas.
