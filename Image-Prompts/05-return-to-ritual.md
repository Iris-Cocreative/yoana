A flat editorial illustration for use as an icon. At the center of the composition, a single candle flame — simple, upward-pointing, slightly organic in shape. Around it, a loose wreath of minimal botanical elements: five or six simplified leaf forms, rendered as pairs of curved lines without detail, arranged in a circular frame. The wreath is spare, not dense — space between each botanical element.

Below the flame, three curved horizontal lines suggest water, earth, or breath — a subtle grounding element. They arc gently across the lower center of the composition.

The whole image feels like a simple ceremonial mark — something you might find on the cover of a handmade program for a grief ritual or a land acknowledgment ceremony.

Background: warm cream (#fff6cf), flat.

Color palette: deep vermillion-earth (#b83518) for the leaf outlines and the three base lines, warm gold (#f4c656) for the flame itself, muted terra cotta (#ff8e3f) for secondary botanical accents. Umber outlines (#4a2418). Background cream. No other colors.

Style: contemporary editorial illustration. Handcrafted quality — slightly imperfect curves that feel drawn, not mechanical. References botanical illustration, sacred geometry, and movement grief ritual aesthetics. Not new-age cliché.

Mood: rest, grounding, ceremony. For the people who carry the world's pain.

Avoid: lotus flower, mandala (cliché), crystals, chakra imagery, photorealism, angel wings, glowing orbs, candle-holder clip art, spa aesthetics, stock wellness iconography, gradients, 3D effects. No generic "self-care" visual language.
